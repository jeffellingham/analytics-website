About Me

I created a short timeline depicting how my life gradually led me toward pursuing a career in Data Analytics.

The Ohio State University

- Spent my first year of college at Wright State University where I won a national championship in hockey.

Kitchen Manager and Family

- Moved to Vail, Colorado after graduating during the recession.
- Worked 2-3 jobs for a couple of years before becoming manager of a restaurant in the heart of Vail Village, mere feet from the ski lifts.
- After managing for a few years, I moved to Florida to be near my parents and last grandparent while seeking a new career.

2010 - 2020
2020 - present

Pandemic Pursuits

- Lost my job when the pandemic hit and took the opportunity to dive into potential careers of interest, starting content marketing.
- However, I ended up writing far more fiction and wrote two sci-fi novels in a series in a matter of months.
- I did a lot of research into artificial intelligence, and therefore data science, while writing the novels. So when I discovered Google's Data Analytics Professional Certificate I tried it out and became enamored with the topics, ideas, and tools.
- I received the professional certificate and also completed hundreds of hours of courses for Excel, Tableau, R, and SQL to supplement and complement it.

Certificates and Courses

Google's Data Analytics Professional Certificate: 180 hours across 8 courses that covered every step of the data analytics process from asking, preparing, and processing/cleaning the data to analyzing, sharing, and acting on it. I learned how analysts use tools like Excel, Sheets, R, Tableau, and SQL, and countless other job-relevant nuggets of knowledge.

Data Visualization with Tableau Specialization: 60 hours across 5 courses, this specialization by UC Davis taught the fundamentals of using Tableau as a visualization tool, as well as design principles, visual analytics, creating dashboards and story point presentations, and a capstone project.

SQL for Data Science: Taught by UC Davis.

Microsoft Excel - Excel from Beginner to Advanced : 21 hours of video and exercises covering everything from the basics to PivotTables, lookup functions (VLOOKUP and INDEX/MATCH), PowerPivot, charts, and the last third covered VBA.

Google Analytics Certification: Completed the Google course, and a supplemental one on Udemy, to become proficient with Google Analytics and receive the certification.
